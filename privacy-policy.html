DATA WE COLLECT

From the LinkedIn Marketing API, after you have granted access, we may collect account and campaign data (for example account ID, account name, campaign and ad names, objectives, targeting settings, budgets and schedules), performance and analytics data (for example impressions, clicks, click through rates, video views, leads, conversions and cost metrics such as CPC, CPM, CPL and CPA, as well as aggregated demographic performance data such as job function, industry, seniority, company size and location when provided by LinkedIn) and lead and conversion data (for example LinkedIn Lead Gen Form submissions including the fields you configure such as name, email, company, job title and phone number, plus LinkedIn Insight Tag events such as page views, sign ups, demo requests and purchases, depending on your configuration).

We do not have access to your LinkedIn username or password. Access is granted through LinkedIns OAuth system.

Through Albato, which we use as an integration and automation tool, we process the same advertising and performance data, lead data from LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms and technical metadata such as timestamps and workflow logs, for the purpose of moving data between LinkedIn and other systems like spreadsheets, CRMs and reporting tools. Albato acts as our sub processor and only processes data needed to run the automations you have requested.

You may also provide data directly to us, such as contact details (name, email address, phone number, billing information), project information (for example reporting requirements, target audiences, KPIs and tracking setups) and access keys or service accounts for third party tools. We never ask for or store your LinkedIn login credentials.

PURPOSES AND LEGAL BASES

We process personal data in order to provide advertising analytics, reporting and optimization services; this includes building dashboards and reports, monitoring performance and integrating LinkedIn Ads data into your internal tools. The legal basis is the performance of a contract with you and our legitimate interest in delivering and improving these services.

We process data to manage our client relationships, including communication, invoicing, accounting and contract management. The legal basis is performance of a contract and compliance with legal obligations.

We may use aggregated or anonymised data to improve our services, models and internal analytics. The legal basis is our legitimate interest in improving and securing our services.

We may process data to comply with applicable laws and regulations and to establish, exercise or defend legal claims. The legal basis is compliance with legal obligations and our legitimate interests.

Where required by law (for example for some types of direct marketing to individuals), we rely on consent. You or your leads can withdraw consent at any time.

HOW WE USE LINKEDIN AND ALBATO

We access LinkedIn Ads data only after you have granted the necessary permissions via LinkedIn. We use the data solely for the purposes agreed with you and in accordance with LinkedIn policies and applicable laws. We do not sell LinkedIn data or share it with unrelated third parties for their own independent marketing.

We use Albato to automate the transfer, transformation and storage of data between LinkedIn and other systems you use, such as Google Sheets, data warehouses, BI tools or CRMs. Albato acts only on our instructions and is bound by a data processing agreement that covers confidentiality, security and compliance.

DATA SHARING

We may share personal data with service providers who help us deliver our services, including Albato, hosting and cloud providers, analytics and reporting tools and professional advisors such as accountants and lawyers. These providers act as processors or sub processors and may only process data according to our instructions.

When we act as a processor, we share data back with the relevant client and their internal teams in the form of reports, dashboards, synced records or exports.

We may disclose data to public authorities or courts when we are legally required to do so or when disclosure is necessary to establish, exercise or defend legal claims.

We do not sell personal data.

INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

Your data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your country of residence, including countries outside the EU or EEA. When personal data is transferred from the EU or EEA to a country that does not provide an adequate level of protection, we use appropriate safeguards such as the European Commissions Standard Contractual Clauses and, where appropriate, additional technical or organisational measures.

DATA RETENTION

We keep personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy. This typically includes the duration of our contract with you, any period during which we provide reporting or support after termination, and any additional period required by law for accounting, tax or legal reasons. We may retain aggregated or anonymised information that does not identify individuals for analytical and statistical purposes.

DATA SECURITY

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data. These measures include access control based on roles, restricted access to API tokens and credentials, encryption in transit and at rest where supported by our providers, logging and monitoring of integrations and periodic review of our key sub processors security practices. Although we take these steps, no system can guarantee absolute security.
